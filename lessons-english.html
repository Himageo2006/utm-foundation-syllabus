Academic Listening & Speaking · FSPE0022

Speak & listen with confidence

Exactly how each assessment works and how to score well — impromptu speech, oral presentation, group discussion and the listening exam.

📘 Assessments

  1. Impromptu Speech (20%)
  2. Oral Presentation (10%)
  3. Group Discussion (10%)
  4. Listening Test & Final Exam (60%)
1

Impromptu Speech — 2×10%

How to organise a speech on the spot▶

You get a current-issue topic and little prep time (2–4 min speech, semi-formal). The skill is organising your thoughts fast and delivering confidently.

A simple 3-part structure

  1. Introduction — state your stance clearly ("I believe that…").
  2. Body — give 1–2 reasons, each with a quick example.
  3. Conclusion — restate your position in one sentence.

What the rubric rewards

  • Organisation — clear intro, body, conclusion.
  • Clarity of ideas — supported points, correct grammar & vocabulary.
  • Delivery — natural (not scripted) tone, eye contact, gestures.
  • Time — aim for 2–3 minutes (best score band).
30-second planning trick: jot Stance → Reason 1 → Reason 2 → Restate on paper, then just talk through it.
2

Oral Presentation — 10%

Delivering a formal informative talk (5–7 min)▶

A prepared, formal talk on a topic related to your field of study, with visual aids.

Structure

  1. Introduction — attention grabber, establish credibility, preview your 2 main points.
  2. Body — main point 1 (supporting detail + example), main point 2 (supporting detail + example).
  3. Conclusion — signal the end, summarise key points.

Scored on

  • Speaking — language, pronunciation, fluency, body language.
  • Content — clear structure, smooth transitions between points.
  • Visual aids — relevant, clear graphics; accurate, concise text.
Slide tip: one idea per slide, big readable text, images over paragraphs. Don't read your slides — talk to them.
3

Group Discussion — 10%

Discussing & reaching a group decision▶

In groups (10–15 min) you discuss a current-issue situation. It runs in two parts: a short individual presentation (Task A), then group interaction to reach a decision (Task B).

Useful language

  • Give opinion: "In my view…", "I'd argue that…"
  • Invite others: "What do you think, …?"
  • Agree/disagree politely: "That's a good point, but…", "I partly agree…"

Scored on

  • Task fulfilment — logical, relevant ideas with evidence.
  • Language — correct discussion expressions & grammar.
  • Interactive ability — responding, taking turns, including others, concluding.
Don't dominate or stay silent — both lose marks. Aim to contribute ~3–4 quality turns and bring quieter members in.
4

Listening Test (2×10%) & Final Exam (40%)

Listening skills & question types▶

Audio-based assessments. Two coursework tests (Units 1–8, 30 min each, via IQ Skills Online) plus a 1-hour final exam.

Skills tested

  • Identify the main idea and supporting details.
  • Make inferences, listen for opinions, spot cause & effect, and predict.

Question types

  • Match key words to definitions
  • Multiple choice
  • Fill in the blanks / information transfer
Strategy: read the questions before the audio plays so you know what to listen for. Note keywords; don't try to write every word.
🎤 Practise speaking out loud — record yourself, or ask the AI Tutor to give you an impromptu topic and feedback on your structure.
