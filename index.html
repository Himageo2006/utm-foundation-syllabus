Academic Listening & Speaking Skills

FSPE0022 · Language Academy, UTMJB

This subject trains you to communicate confidently in academic English — speaking spontaneously, presenting formally, discussing in groups, and listening for meaning. Your grade is built almost entirely from performance tasks plus a final listening exam.

Spoken English Presentations Group discussion Listening comprehension

📋 How you'll be assessed

AssessmentWeightFormatDetails
Impromptu Speech 2 × 10% = 20% Individual Speak on a current issue — 2 to 4 min, semi-formal. Intro (stance) → body (opinion) → conclusion.
Oral Presentation 10% Individual Informative talk on your field of study — 5 to 7 min, formal. Graded on speaking, content & visual aids.
Group Discussion 10% Group 10–15 min per group. Present a view, support it with evidence, agree/disagree respectfully, take turns.
Listening Test 2 × 10% = 20% Individual Audio-based, Units 1–8 of the textbook. 30 min each. Done via IQ Skills Online.
Final Examination 40% Individual 1-hour listening exam — main idea, detail, inference, opinion, cause & effect, prediction.
Total100%Coursework (60%) + Final Exam (40%)

💡 Tip: Speaking tasks are marked on organisation, clarity of ideas, delivery (eye contact, tone, gestures) and time management. Practising out loud — not just in your head — makes the biggest difference.

Calculus

Mathematics · Semester 3

Calculus is the mathematics of change and accumulation. You'll start with limits, learn to differentiate and integrate, then apply these tools to real problems — finishing with differential equations and numerical methods. Each topic builds on the last, so don't fall behind.

TOPIC 1
Limits & Continuity
The foundation: what a function approaches, and where it "breaks".
TOPIC 2
Differentiation
Rates of change and the rules for finding derivatives.
TOPIC 3
Applications of Differentiation
Maxima/minima, optimisation and curve behaviour.
TOPIC 4
Integration
The reverse of differentiation — finding areas and totals.
TOPIC 5
Integration of Trigonometric Functions
Techniques for integrating sin, cos, tan and friends.
TOPIC 6
Applications of Integration
Area between curves, volumes and accumulated quantities.
TOPIC 7
Ordinary Differential Equations
Equations involving derivatives — modelling growth & motion.
TOPIC 8
Numerical Methods
Approximating answers when exact solutions are hard.

Chemistry II

Physical & Organic Chemistry · Semester 3

Chemistry II focuses on energy, change and reactions — how much heat reactions release, how fast they go, where they settle, and how acids and bases behave — before opening the door to organic chemistry.

CHAPTER 1
Thermochemistry
Heat changes in reactions — enthalpy, Hess's law.
CHAPTER 2
Chemical Kinetics
Reaction rates and the factors that speed them up or slow them down.
CHAPTER 3
Chemical Equilibrium
When forward and reverse reactions balance — Le Chatelier's principle.
CHAPTER 4
Acids & Bases
pH, strength, neutralisation and buffer systems.
CHAPTER 6
Organic Chemistry
The chemistry of carbon compounds — functional groups & reactions.

📝 Based on the lecture slides available. Confirm the exact chapter list (and any chapter 5 content) with your lecturer's course outline.

How to succeed this semester

Practical advice for foundation students
🗓️

Coursework adds up

Most subjects spread marks across tests, presentations and quizzes. Small consistent effort beats last-minute cramming.

🔗

Topics build on each other

In Calculus and Physics especially, each chapter depends on the previous one. Never skip the basics.

🗣️

Practise speaking aloud

For English, rehearse presentations and impromptu speeches out loud — confidence and timing are graded.

💻

Code every week

For Computing, write and run small C programs regularly. You learn programming by doing, not reading.

❓

Ask early

Confused by a topic? Ask your lecturer or coursemates the same week — gaps only get harder to fix later.

📚

Use the slides

Your lecture slides are your primary study material. Review them before and after each class.
