Descriptive Statistics

Mean, median, mode & standard deviation▶
  • Mean = sum of values ÷ how many.
  • Median = the middle value when sorted.
  • Mode = the most frequent value.
  • Standard deviation = how spread out the data is.
WORKED EXAMPLE

Find the mean and median of: 4, 8, 6, 5, 2.

1Mean = (4+8+6+5+2)/5 = 25/5 = 5
2Sort: 2, 4, 5, 6, 8 → middle value = 5 (median).
Standard deviation σ = √( Σ(x − x̄)² / n )

Probability

Basic probability rules▶
P(event) = favourable outcomes / total outcomes   (between 0 and 1)
  • Addition (OR): P(A or B) = P(A) + P(B) − P(A and B).
  • Multiplication (AND, independent): P(A and B) = P(A)·P(B).
  • Complement: P(not A) = 1 − P(A).
WORKED EXAMPLE

A fair die is rolled. Find P(rolling an even number).

1Even outcomes = {2, 4, 6} → 3 favourable. Total = 6.
2P = 3/6 = 1/2

Probability Distributions

Binomial & normal distributions▶

Binomial — for n independent trials each with success probability p:

P(X = r) = ⁿCᵣ · pʳ · (1−p)ⁿ⁻ʳ

Normal — the bell curve, described by mean μ and standard deviation σ. Convert to the standard normal with the z-score:

z = (x − μ) / σ
WORKED EXAMPLE — z-score

Marks are normally distributed with μ = 60, σ = 10. Find the z-score for a mark of 75.

1z = (75 − 60)/10
2= 15/10 = 1.5 (the mark is 1.5 standard deviations above the mean).

Data Analysis

Presenting & interpreting data▶

Organise data with frequency tables, then visualise it:

  • Bar chart — compare categories.
  • Histogram — show distribution of continuous data.
  • Pie chart — show proportions of a whole.
  • Scatter plot — show relationship between two variables (correlation).
Interpreting: look for the centre (average), the spread (range/SD), and the shape (symmetric? skewed? outliers?).
