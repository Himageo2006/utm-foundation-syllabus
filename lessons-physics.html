Electrostatics

1.1 вҖ” Static electricity, charge & its conservationв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: Rub a balloon on your hair and it sticks to the wall вҖ” that's static electricity. Objects carry tiny amounts of charge, either positive or negative. The rule: opposites attract, sames repel. And charge is never created or destroyed вҖ” it only moves from one place to another.

Objects can be charged by rubbing. Charge comes in two types, positive and negative; like charges repel and opposite charges attract.

Law of conservation of electric charge: the net amount of electric charge produced in any process is zero вҖ” no net electric charge can be created or destroyed. The arithmetic sum of the total charge cannot change in any interaction.

Q = ne  В·  e = 1.61Г—10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C  В·  Вө = Г—10вҒ»вҒ¶  n = Г—10вҒ»вҒ№
Charge is quantized in units of e (charge on the electron e = 1.602Г—10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C).
1.1.1 вҖ” Insulators and conductorsв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: A conductor lets charge move through it easily (metals вҖ” think copper wires). An insulator blocks it (plastic, wood, rubber). That's literally why wires are metal inside and plastic outside.

Conductor: a material in which charge flows freely. The most common conductors are metals.

Insulator: a material in which almost no charge flows. Most non-metallic materials are insulators.

Two spheres connected by a metal nail (conductor) share charge from one to the other; connected by wood (insulator), almost no charge is conducted.
1.1.2 вҖ” Induced charge; the electroscopeв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: You can charge something without even touching it: bring a charged object close and it pushes/pulls the charges already inside, making one end positive and the other negative ("induced" charge). An electroscope is a simple gadget whose metal leaves spread apart when charged вҖ” a charge detector.

Metal objects can be charged by conduction (charge by contact) and by induction (either while connected to ground or not).

  • A charged object brought near a neutral metal rod causes a separation of charge вҖ” the rod stays neutral overall.
  • Nonconductors won't become charged by conduction or induction, but will experience charge separation.

The electroscope (metal knob вҶ’ rod вҶ’ gold leaves, in a glass case) can be used for detecting charge, and a charged electroscope can determine the sign of an unknown charge.

1.2 вҖ” Coulomb's lawв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: Coulomb's law is the formula for how strongly two charges push or pull each other. Bigger charges вҶ’ stronger force; further apart вҶ’ much weaker force (it drops with distance squared, so double the distance = quarter the force). It's basically gravity's electric cousin.

The electric force between two charges is proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between them. It acts along the line connecting them вҖ” attractive if opposite, repulsive if the same.

1.3.1 / 1.3.2 вҖ” Field lines & conductorsв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: We draw electric fields as arrows/lines flowing from + charges to вҲ’ charges. Where the lines bunch up close together, the field is strong. Two handy facts: the lines never cross, and inside a metal conductor the field is zero (charges sit on the outside surface).

The electric field can be drawn as field lines that start on positive charges and end on negative charges. Characteristics:

  1. Lines indicate the field direction (tangent to the line at any point).
  2. Field magnitude вҲқ number of lines per unit area вҖ” the closer the lines, the stronger the field.
  3. Lines start on + and end on вҲ’; their number вҲқ magnitude of the charge.
  4. Field lines never cross (the field can't have two values at one point).

Electric fields and conductors: the static field inside a conductor is zero (else charges would move); net charge resides on the outer surface; and the field is perpendicular to the surface.

Between two oppositely charged parallel plates the field is uniform вҖ” parallel, equally spaced lines from + plate to вҲ’ plate in the central region.
1.4 вҖ” Electric potential due to point chargesв–¶

2.1 вҖ” Capacitors and capacitanceв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: A capacitor is a little energy tank вҖ” two metal plates that store electric charge, ready to release it in a burst (like a camera flash). "Capacitance" just measures how much charge it can hold per volt вҖ” a bigger number means a bigger tank.

A capacitor is a device capable of storing electric charge or electric potential energy вҖ” two conducting plates separated by a small air gap or a thin insulator (a dielectric such as mica, ceramics, paper or oil). Plates may be cylindrical, spherical or parallel.

Capacitance C is the ratio of the charge on either plate to the potential difference between them. It is a scalar, always positive; unit farad (F) = CВ·VвҒ»В№.

C = Q/V  вҶ’  Q = CV  (Q вҲқ V)  В·  1 ВөF = 10вҒ»вҒ¶ F, 1 pF = 10вҒ»В№ВІ F

2.2 вҖ” Capacitors in series and parallelв–¶

2.3 вҖ” Electric energy storageв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: Charging a capacitor stores energy (you did work pushing charge onto it). Three equivalent formulas give that stored energy вҖ” pick whichever matches the info you have (Q, C, or V). That stored energy is what a defibrillator or camera flash dumps out all at once.

A charged capacitor stores energy equal to the work done to charge it:

U = ВҪQВІ/C = ВҪCVВІ = ВҪQV

Sudden discharge can be harmful or fatal вҖ” capacitors retain charge even when disconnected. Heart defibrillators use this discharge to restart the heart.

2.4 вҖ” Dielectricsв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: A dielectric is an insulating slab (plastic, mica, paper) slipped between the plates. It boosts the capacitance вҖ” letting the capacitor store more charge and handle higher voltage in a smaller size. That's why real capacitors aren't just two plates in air.

A dielectric is a non-conducting material placed between the plates. Inserting it increases C by the dielectric constant Оө_r (Оә). Advantages: more capacitance, higher operating voltage, mechanical support (smaller d, larger C).

Dielectric strength = the field at which breakdown occurs and the material becomes a conductor. Battery connected вҶ’ V constant, C and Q rise. Battery disconnected вҶ’ Q constant, V drops.

Magnetism

5.1 вҖ” Magnets and Magnetic Fieldsв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: Magnets have a north and south pole вҖ” likes repel, opposites attract (just like charges). A magnetic field is the region around a magnet where this force is felt, drawn as lines from N to S. Fun fact: you can never get a lone pole вҖ” snap a magnet in two and each piece grows its own N and S.

Magnets have two ends вҖ” poles вҖ” called north (N) and south (S). Like poles repel; unlike poles attract.

If you cut a magnet in half, you don't get a separate north pole and south pole вҖ” you get two smaller magnets (each with both N and S). A single magnetic pole has never been found; poles always come in pairs.

The Earth's magnetic field is similar to that of a bar magnet. Note that the Earth's geographic "North Pole" is really a south magnetic pole, since the north ends of magnets are attracted to it.

Magnetic field is defined as a region around a magnet where a magnetic force can be experienced.

  • A stationary electric charge is surrounded by an electric field only.
  • When an electric charge moves, it is surrounded by both an electric field and a magnetic field вҖ” the motion of the charge produces the magnetic field.

Magnetic field lines represent a magnetic field. By convention, field lines leave the north pole and enter the south pole. The tangent to a curved field line at a point gives the direction of the field at that point. The pattern can be shown using compass needles or by sprinkling iron filings on paper.

A neutral point is a point where the resultant magnetic force is zero (e.g. between two like poles).

Electromagnetic Induction

6.1 вҖ” Induced EMFв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: We saw electricity makes magnetism вҖ” now the reverse: a changing magnetic field makes electricity. Wave a magnet near a coil and you generate a voltage. The catch is it must be changing вҖ” a still magnet does nothing; faster movement makes more voltage. This is the idea behind every generator.

Faraday looked for evidence that a magnetic field induces a current using a coil, battery, switch and galvanometer. He found no current when the current was steady, but a current was induced when the switch was turned on or off.

Moving a bar magnet relative to a coil:

  • Magnet stationary вҶ’ galvanometer shows no deflection (no current).
  • Magnet moved towards the coil вҶ’ momentary deflection one way; moved away вҶ’ deflects the opposite way.

Conclusion: when the magnetic field lines through a coil change, an induced emf exists across the coil. The magnitude depends on the speed of relative motion вҖ” v increases вҶ’ induced emf increases (induced emf вҲқ v).

6.2.1 вҖ” Magnetic fluxв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: Flux is just "how many field lines pass through a loop." It's biggest when the loop faces the field straight-on and zero when the loop is edge-on. Changing this flux is exactly what creates induced voltage вҖ” so flux is the key quantity for the rest of this chapter.

Magnetic flux is the scalar product of the magnetic flux density B and the area vector A:

ОҰ = BВ·A = BAВ·cosП•  (unit: weber, Wb = TВ·mВІ)

П• is the angle between B and the area vector A (which is always normal to the surface). ОҰ is maximum (= BA) when П• = 0В° (B вҠҘ surface, parallel to A), and zero when П• = 90В°. Flux вҲқ number of field lines through the area.

6.2 вҖ” Faraday's Law of Inductionв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: Faraday's law puts a number on it: the faster the flux changes, the bigger the voltage (and more coil turns multiply it). You can change the flux three ways вҖ” change the field strength, the loop's area, or its angle. The minus sign is just Lenz's law (next lesson) reminding us the effect opposes the change.

Оө = вҲ’dОҰ/dt  and for N turns  Оө = вҲ’NВ·dОҰ/dt = вҲ’N(ОҰ_f вҲ’ ОҰ_i)/dt

6.2.2 вҖ” Lenz's Lawв–¶
рҹҹў In plain words: Lenz's law tells you which way the induced current flows: it always opposes whatever change created it ("nature pushes back"). Push a magnet in, the coil pushes it away; pull it out, the coil tries to hold it. It's really just energy conservation вҖ” you have to do work to generate electricity.

Lenz's law: an induced emf is always in a direction that opposes the original change in flux that caused it (the current produces a field that tends to restore the changed flux). It is used to find the direction of the induced current.

To produce an induced current you need a closed conducting loop and an external flux through it that changes in time. Steps:

  1. Decide whether ОҰ_B = BAВ·cosОё is increasing, decreasing or unchanged.
  2. B_induced: if ОҰ_B is decreasing вҶ’ same direction as external field; if increasing вҶ’ opposite direction; if unchanged вҶ’ zero.
  3. Use the right-hand rule on B_induced to get the induced current direction.

6.5 вҖ” Transformers & power transmissionв–¶

V_S/V_P = N_S/N_P  and  I_S/I_P = N_P/N_S  |  P = VI

6.6 вҖ” Self-Inductanceв–¶

7.1 вҖ” Electromagnetic Wavesв–¶

10-1 Huygens' Principle & Law of Refractionв–¶

Wavefront: a line or surface, in the path of a wave motion, on which the disturbances at every point have the same phase. A wavefront is always perpendicular to the direction of wave propagation.

The bending of waves behind obstacles is called diffraction. Diffraction occurs (a) around the edge of an obstacle, (b) through a large hole, (c) through a small hole whose size is on the order of the wavelength.

Highway mirages arise from a gradually changing index of refraction in heated air (hot air near the road is less dense вҶ’ lower index вҶ’ light bends and seems reflected off the road).

Early Quantum Theory & Models of the Atom

11-1 Planck's Quantum Hypothesis; Blackbody Radiationв–¶

A black body is an ideal system that absorbs all radiation incident on it; the radiation it emits is black-body radiation. Total intensity is proportional to the fourth power of temperature: I = P/A = ПғeTвҒҙ.

12-1 Structure & Properties of the Nucleusв–¶

A nucleus is made of protons and neutrons (collectively nucleons). Proton charge +e (1.60Г—10вҒ»В№вҒ№ C), mass 1.672Г—10вҒ»ВІвҒ· kg; neutron uncharged, mass 1.675Г—10вҒ»ВІвҒ· kg. In a neutral atom, number of protons = number of electrons.

1 MeV = 1.60Г—10вҒ»В№Ві J. The higher the binding energy per nucleon, the more stable the nucleus (вҒөвҒ¶Fe is near the peak). Nucleons are held by the short-range strong nuclear force (вүҲ0 beyond ~10вҒ»В№вҒө m); the long-range Coulomb force is why high-Z nuclei need extra neutrons. The weak nuclear force governs beta decay.

A nuclear reaction occurs when a nucleus is struck by another nucleus or particle. If the original nucleus is transformed into another, it is called transmutation вҖ” e.g. вҒҙвӮӮHe + В№вҒҙвӮҮN вҶ’ В№вҒ·вӮҲO + В№вӮҒH. Energy and momentum are conserved; ОЈZ and ОЈA are conserved.

13-2 Nuclear Fission & Reactorsв–¶

Nuclear fission: a heavy nucleus splits into two lighter nuclei of nearly equal mass, emitting neutrons and energy. A reactor keeps it self-sustaining but controlled: a moderator (heavy water, graphite) slows neutrons; natural uranium (0.7% fissionable) is usually enriched to 2вҖ“3%. Below the critical mass too many neutrons escape and the chain stops. An atomic bomb is a deliberate uncontrolled chain reaction.

13-3 Nuclear Fusionв–¶

Nuclear fusion: two light nuclei fuse into a heavier nucleus, releasing large energy (thermonuclear energy). Because both nuclei are positive, very high kinetic energies (temperatures ~10вҒё K) are needed to overcome Coulomb repulsion; reactants form a plasma. Fusion occurs in fusion bombs and stellar cores (the Sun fuses H вҶ’ He: net 4В№вӮҒH вҶ’ вҒҙвӮӮHe + 2eвҒә + 2ОҪ + 2Оі).

Fission vs fusion: fission splits a heavy nucleus, occurs at controllable temperature, easier to sustain; fusion combines light nuclei, needs ~10вҒё K, hard to sustain. Both produce energy. On the binding-energy-per-nucleon curve, the rising part (low A) favours fusion, the falling part (high A) favours fission; вҒөвҒ¶Fe is the most stable.
